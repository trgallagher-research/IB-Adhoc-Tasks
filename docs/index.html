Problem 1
Using MM to evidence complex competencies is time-intensive for teachers, which limits how often and consistently it can be done.
Strawman concept: Competency Crumbs. Instead of periodic, comprehensive assessment across all competencies, a teacher captures one small observation per week. Over a term, these accumulate into a usable evidence picture.

Formative Assessment — Competency Crumbs

This is a deliberately rough strawman for conversation, not a finished design. The question is whether sparse, distributed evidence can produce MM-quality signal at a fraction of the evidence burden.
1
One observation per week
Teacher picks ONE student and ONE competency. Writes 2–3 sentences using shared MM descriptor language. Takes ~3 minutes.
2
Evidence accumulates
Over a 10-week term, each student in a class of 25 gets ~10 observations spread across competencies. No single assessment event needed.
3
Fortnightly moderation
Teachers bring a handful of crumbs to a 20-minute staff conversation. Calibrate descriptor use. No formal marking sessions.
Target: MM-quality signal from <20% of the current evidence burden.

Fortnightly moderation (staff conversation)

1
Select crumbs
Each teacher brings 2–3 crumbs from the past fortnight. Focus on ones they found hard to level.
→
2
Calibrate
Read the observation aloud. Others say what level they'd give. Discuss differences. 5 minutes per crumb.
→
3
Adjust
Teacher updates the level in the log if the group consensus differs. No formal paperwork.
What this addresses: The current MM approach requires comprehensive evidence across all competencies for each student, creating a substantial time burden. Competency Crumbs inverts the model: one observation, one student, one competency, once a week. Evidence accumulates gradually over a term. Moderation is short and conversational rather than formal. The open question is whether this sparse, distributed evidence is still valid evidence, or whether it crosses the line from formative into anecdotal.

Draft RFP shown for illustration. Budget range and eligibility criteria would be confirmed before issue.

Request for Proposal:
Scoping Study on MM Applicability Across Diverse School Contexts

1. Background

MM is a framework for evidencing complex student competencies developed through a partnership between IB and a partner university. To date, the framework has been tested in a cohort of 28 schools. These schools are predominantly well-resourced, English-medium institutions. Before IB can consider broader adoption of MM, it needs to understand whether the framework is applicable, adaptable, or viable across the full range of IB schools, including schools with different resourcing levels, languages of instruction, and regulatory environments.

2. Research Questions

  1. RQ1: In what school contexts does MM work well with minimal adaptation?
  2. RQ2: In what contexts does MM require significant adaptation, and what form would those adaptations take?
  3. RQ3: Are there school contexts where MM is unlikely to be viable, and what are the defining characteristics of those contexts?

3. Sample Requirements

  • Minimum 10 schools across at least 3 regions
  • Must include: schools serving lower-socioeconomic communities (eligibility criteria to be confirmed with Research)
  • Must include: government-funded or government-assisted schools
  • Must include: schools where the primary language of instruction is not English
  • Must include: schools not in the current 28-school MM cohort
  • Desirable: range of programme levels (Primary, Middle Years, Diploma)

4. Methodology

The study should employ mixed methods, including classroom observation, teacher interviews, and document analysis of MM evidence portfolios. Proposals should specify their methodological approach and justify their design choices.

5. Deliverables

  • Interim report (6 months from contract signature)
  • Final report with recommendations
  • Anonymised data set
  • Executive summary for the board

6. Timeline

12–18 months from contract signature.

7. Budget

Proposals should include a detailed budget. Indicative scope and budget envelope to be confirmed before issue.

8. Submission

What this addresses: MM has been tested primarily in middle-class school contexts. This sketch produces a procurement-ready artefact that IB can use to commission a scoping study investigating applicability across the broader range of IB schools.
