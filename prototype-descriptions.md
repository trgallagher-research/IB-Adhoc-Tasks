# Melbourne Metrics — Prototype Descriptions

Five prototype sketches responding to problem statements from the Melbourne Metrics work. Produced by the Learning Lab, April 2026.

---

## Problem statements

1. Using MM to evidence complex competencies is time-intensive for teachers, which limits how often and consistently it can be done.
2. MM adds to teacher workload in a context of wider pressure on teacher wellbeing, raising questions about sustainability of use.
3. Using MM well requires professional learning that IB does not currently provide, leaving schools to develop capability on their own.
4. MM has been tested primarily in middle-class school contexts, so its applicability across the broader range of IB schools is not established.

---

## Problem 1 — Two complementary prototypes

Two complementary approaches: event-anchored (Exhibition Review Form uses an existing event, the PYP Exhibition) and planning-anchored (Unit Planner embeds evidence capture in routine unit planning).

### Core & Coherence — MM Exhibition Review Form

**What it is:** A single-page MM observation and self-assessment form designed for PYP Exhibition presentations. The form maps MM competency descriptors to observable behaviours during exhibition. Four versions share the same structure and descriptors: teacher assessment, student self-assessment, peer assessment, and parent/community observer form. Teachers carry the form during exhibition presentations and record evidence in real time.

**MM competencies assessed:** Acting Ethically, Active Citizenship, Agency in Learning, Character, Collaboration, Communication. Each competency uses MM's 7-level empirical progression (Level 1 lowest to Level 7 highest), with a nutshell descriptor at each level.

**Form structure:**
- Header: student name, exhibition title, date, assessor role
- Per competency: evidence observed (open text) + level judgement (1–7 radio selection with nutshell descriptors)
- Footer: overall comment, assessor name, signature, date
- Four assessor versions (Teacher / Student self / Peer / Parent-Community) share the identical form structure

**Who it is for:** PYP Exhibition coordinators, presenting students, supervising teachers, parents, and community members attending exhibition.

**What it would do:** Generates MM evidence from an event that already happens in every PYP school. Multiple assessor perspectives on the same descriptors produce richer evidence with no additional scheduling. The shared form structure makes cross-assessor moderation straightforward. Extends naturally to Personal Project, Community Project, and other core presentations.

**Limitation:** This sketch is PYP-only. Equivalent anchor events (Personal Project, Community Project, extended essay) would need their own form versions.

**Caveats:** The 7-level progression shown uses real MM competency names with illustrative nutshell descriptors. Final form would use MM's actual level-descriptor language from the New Metrics empirical progressions. Different MM competencies use different scales; this form uses a common 7-level layout for illustration only.

### Curriculum Development — MM-Integrated Unit Planner

**What it is:** A modified IB unit planner template (DP format) with additional MM evidence columns. For each assessment task in the unit, the planner prompts teachers to identify which MM competency descriptors the task could evidence and what form that evidence takes. Uses standard IB language (command terms, assessment objectives, ATL skills). Prototype shown for DP Economics.

**Planner columns (existing IB columns):**
- Inquiry question
- Content / topic
- ATL skills
- Assessment task
- IB command terms

**New MM columns (additions):**
- MM competency descriptors evidenced (e.g. "Agency in Learning, Level 4–5; Acting Ethically, Level 3–4")
- Evidence form (e.g. "Written (submitted task)", "Oral (observed) + Written (position paper)")

**Worked example (DP Economics, Market Failure unit):**

| Inquiry question | Assessment task | MM descriptors | Evidence form |
|---|---|---|---|
| Why do markets fail to allocate resources efficiently? | Written analysis of a local externality using real data | Agency in Learning (Level 4–5); Acting Ethically (Level 3–4) | Written (submitted task) |
| Who should bear the cost of market failure? | Group policy debate with position paper | Communication (Level 4–5); Collaboration (Level 4–5) | Oral (observed) + Written (position paper) |
| How do we measure what markets cannot price? | Individual research report on a common pool resource problem in a different national context | Agency in Learning (Level 5); Active Citizenship (Level 4) | Written (submitted task) |

**Evidence workflow:** Unit planner (identifies where evidence will be captured) → Classroom activity (teachers run the task; student work generates evidence) → Ruby (evidence uploaded to MM platform for judgement against descriptors and internal moderation).

**Who it is for:** DP subject teachers and curriculum development teams working on subject reviews.

**What it would do:** Embeds MM evidence capture into planning teachers already do. Makes competency evidence a design consideration at unit level rather than an add-on after the fact. Gives curriculum reviewers a template they can include in subject guides so that MM alignment is built into specifications, not left to schools.

**Caveats:** Worked example shown for DP Economics. Planner columns and content are illustrative.

## Problem 2 — Research: Wellbeing & Sustainability Dashboard

**What it is:** A school-level dashboard that tracks four metrics over time, drawing on two realistic data sources: Ruby platform telemetry (activity volume) and a short termly staff survey (perceived time investment, perceived workload burden, and WHO-5 wellbeing).

**Dashboard panels:**

1. **Activity volume** (source: Ruby platform telemetry). Evidence items uploaded per teacher per month. Shows whether MM is actually being done at the school.
2. **Perceived time investment** (source: termly staff survey). Likert scale, 1 (none) to 5 (very heavy). How heavy does MM feel?
3. **Perceived workload burden** (source: termly staff survey). Likert scale, 1 (very manageable) to 5 (unsustainable). Amber threshold at 3.5, red at 4.0.
4. **Staff wellbeing — WHO-5 Wellbeing Index** (source: termly staff survey). Validated 5-item instrument, 0–100 scale. WHO-5 threshold: 50 indicates need for attention. Designed to integrate with IB wellbeing measurement work currently in development.

**Multi-school comparison view:** Four fictionalised schools plus cohort average, showing different implementation trajectories:
- **Greenfield International** — stable, low-to-moderate activity, wellbeing steady
- **Riverside Academy** — gradual ramp, amber flag on workload burden, then stabilised after school leadership intervention
- **Summit International** — high activity, workload burden and WHO-5 wellbeing crossed thresholds for two consecutive collection points (red alert)
- **Horizon College** — stable, moderate activity, no flags
- **Cohort average** — benchmark line (dashed)

**Alert system:** Red alert triggered when workload burden and WHO-5 wellbeing cross threshold for two consecutive collection points. Recommends follow-up with school leadership.

**Data sources summary:** Ruby provides activity data. A short termly staff survey (5 questions) provides perceived time, burden, and WHO-5 wellbeing. No new data infrastructure required. All metrics aggregated at school level; no individual teacher data displayed.

**Who it is for:** School leaders implementing MM, IB programme managers, and the MM Working Group.

**What it would do:** Provides ongoing monitoring of whether MM implementation is sustainable at school level. Flags schools where indicators are trending toward unsustainable levels so that conversations with school leadership can happen early. Feeds into IB's broader wellbeing measurement platform work.

**Methodological caveat:** This dashboard flags where indicators move together; it does not establish MM as the cause of wellbeing changes. Attribution requires separate analysis.

**Caveats:** All chart data is simulated for illustration. Thresholds shown are placeholders.

## Problem 3 — EDD: Micro-Credential Stack

**What it is:** A three-tier professional learning product for teachers in schools using MM.

**Tier 1 (Free): MM Micro-Lessons**
- 6–8 modules, approximately 15 minutes each, asynchronous
- Open access, no login required
- Modules: What is Melbourne Metrics? | Understanding MM Descriptors | Evidence Types and Sources | Recognising Competencies in Student Work | Using Descriptor Language | MM across PYP, MYP and DP

**Tier 2 (Paid): MM Micro-Credentials**
- Each credential: asynchronous content + 1 facilitated session + assessed task
- Individual enrolment or school purchase
- MC1: Evidence Capture (~USD 50, TBD) — Identify, gather, and document MM-aligned evidence from classroom activities.
- MC2: Judgement Against Descriptors (~USD 50, TBD) — Apply MM descriptors to student evidence and produce defensible judgements.
- MC3: Internal Moderation (~USD 50, TBD) — Lead and participate in school-level moderation of MM judgements.

**Tier 3 (Paid, Bundle): MM Practitioner Credential**
- Bundles all three micro-credentials + facilitated cohort sessions
- ~USD 100/teacher (TBD) — buy two MCs, get one free
- For schools committing to full MM implementation

**Pricing summary:**

| Product | Format | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| MM Micro-Lessons (Tier 1) | 6–8 async modules, ~15 min each | Free | Open access, no login |
| MC1: Evidence Capture | Async + 1 facilitated session + assessed task | ~USD 50 (TBD) | Individual or school purchase |
| MC2: Judgement Against Descriptors | Async + 1 facilitated session + assessed task | ~USD 50 (TBD) | Individual or school purchase |
| MC3: Internal Moderation | Async + 1 facilitated session + assessed task | ~USD 50 (TBD) | Individual or school purchase |
| MM Practitioner Credential (Tier 3) | MC1 + MC2 + MC3 + cohort sessions | ~USD 100 (TBD) | Buy two MCs, get one free |

**Who it is for:** Classroom teachers and coordinators in IB schools using or considering MM. Schools purchase credentials; individuals access micro-lessons freely.

**What it would do:** Creates a visible, scalable IB professional learning offer for MM. The free tier builds awareness and reduces the "schools figure it out themselves" pattern. The paid tier generates revenue and funds ongoing development. Tiered structure lets schools buy what they need.

**Caveats:** Pricing shown is indicative. Actual pricing TBD.

## Problem 4 — Research: Request for Proposal

**What it is:** A draft request for proposal (RFP) for a scoping study on MM applicability across a broader range of IB school contexts. Ready to enter IB procurement processes.

**RFP sections:**

**1. Background.** Melbourne Metrics is a framework for evidencing complex student competencies developed through a partnership between the IB and the University of Melbourne. To date, the framework has been tested in a cohort of 28 schools. These schools are predominantly well-resourced, English-medium institutions. Before the IB can consider broader adoption of MM, it needs to understand whether the framework is applicable, adaptable, or viable across the full range of IB World Schools, including schools with different resourcing levels, languages of instruction, and regulatory environments.

**2. Research questions.**
- RQ1: In what school contexts does MM work well with minimal adaptation?
- RQ2: In what contexts does MM require significant adaptation, and what form would those adaptations take?
- RQ3: Are there school contexts where MM is unlikely to be viable, and what are the defining characteristics of those contexts?

**3. Sample requirements.**
- Minimum 10 schools across at least 3 IB regions
- Must include: schools serving lower-socioeconomic communities (eligibility criteria to be confirmed with Research)
- Must include: government-funded or government-assisted schools
- Must include: schools where the primary language of instruction is not English
- Must include: schools not in the current 28-school MM cohort
- Desirable: range of programme levels (PYP, MYP, DP)

**4. Methodology.** The study should employ mixed methods, including classroom observation, teacher interviews, and document analysis of MM evidence portfolios. Proposals should specify their methodological approach and justify their design choices.

**5. Deliverables.** Interim report (6 months from contract signature), final report with recommendations, anonymised data set, executive summary for the IB Board.

**6. Timeline.** 12–18 months from contract signature.

**7. Budget.** Proposals should include a detailed budget. Indicative scope and budget envelope to be confirmed before issue.

**8. Submission.** Proposals due [date TBC]. Contact: [TBC].

**Who it is for:** The MM Working Group and IB Procurement, for commissioning external research partners. Outputs serve the IB Board and schools considering MM adoption.

**What it would do:** Moves the scoping study from concept to procurement-ready document. Provides a concrete artefact that can go through IB approval processes and be issued to research partners. Grounds the Phase 2 scaling decision in commissioned evidence rather than extrapolation from a well-resourced cohort.

**Caveats:** Draft RFP shown for illustration. Budget range and eligibility criteria would be confirmed before issue.
